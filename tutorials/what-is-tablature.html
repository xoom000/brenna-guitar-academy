üìö What is Tablature?

Guitar tablature (or "tab") is a simple way to read guitar music. Instead of learning traditional music notation, tablature shows you exactly where to put your fingers on the guitar.

üé∏ How to Read Tablature

Each line represents a guitar string, and numbers show which frets to press:

E |-------3-------
B |-------0-------
G |-------0-------
D |-------2-------
A |-------2-------
E |-------0-------

This shows you playing an E minor chord:

  • 0 = Play the string "open" (don't press any fret)
  • 2 = Press down on the 2nd fret
  • 3 = Press down on the 3rd fret

ü§ò Why Tablature is Perfect for Metal

Traditional music notation is complicated and slow to learn. Tablature gets you playing real metal songs immediately!

üî• Simple Power Chord Example

E |----------------
B |----------------
G |----------------
D |-------2--------
A |-------2--------

This is an E5 power chord - the foundation of metal guitar! You only need to press 2 frets and it sounds massive.

üí° Pro Tip

Power chords use just 2-3 strings and create that chunky, aggressive sound you hear in every metal song!

‚ö° Reading Direction & Timing

Tablature reads from left to right, just like reading text. Numbers that are lined up vertically are played at the same time.

üéµ Sequential Notes vs Chords

E |-------0---3---5---
B |----------------
G |----------------
D |----------------
A |----------------
E |----------------

Sequential: Play 0, then 3, then 5 (one after another)

E |-------0--------
B |-------0--------
G |-------0--------
D |-------2--------
A |-------2--------
E |-------0--------

Chord: Play all the numbers at the same time
