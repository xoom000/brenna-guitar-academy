How to Hold a Guitar
Get comfortable with your instrument

Proper Guitar Posture

Before you can rock out, you need to get comfortable holding your guitar. Good posture prevents fatigue and injury while making it easier to play.

  • Sitting: Rest the guitar on your right leg (if right-handed)
  • Back straight: Don't slouch - your back should be upright
  • Guitar angle: Neck should point slightly upward
  • Fretting hand: Thumb behind the neck, fingers curved
  • Picking hand: Relaxed, wrist straight
Proper sitting position
Remember: Comfort is key! If it hurts, you're probably doing something wrong. Take breaks and adjust your position.

Reading Tablature (Tabs)
Your roadmap to playing songs

What is Tablature?

Tablature (or "tabs") is a simple way to read guitar music. It shows you exactly where to put your fingers on the guitar neck.

  • 6 lines: Each line represents a guitar string
  • Numbers: Tell you which fret to press
  • 0: Means play the open string (don't press any fret)
  • Time: Read from left to right

String Order (from top to bottom)

  • E (1st string - thinnest, highest pitch)
  • B (2nd string)
  • G (3rd string)
  • D (4th string)
  • A (5th string)
  • E (6th string - thickest, lowest pitch)

Guitar Tuning
Getting your guitar ready to play

Standard Tuning

Your guitar needs to be in tune to sound good. Standard tuning means each string is tuned to a specific note.

  • 6th string (thickest): E (low E)
  • 5th string: A
  • 4th string: D
  • 3rd string: G
  • 2nd string: B
  • 1st string (thinnest): E (high E)

Memory Trick

"Eddie Ate Dynamite, Good Bye Eddie"

This phrase helps you remember the string notes from lowest to highest!
