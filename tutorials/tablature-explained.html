Your First Step into Reading Guitar Tabs

Learn to read the language of metal guitar - one fret at a time!

üî• What is Tablature?

Tablature (or "tab") is like a roadmap for your guitar. Instead of complicated music notes, tab shows you exactly where to put your fingers on the fretboard. It's the fastest way to learn songs!

üí° Why Tabs Rock for Metal Guitar:

Most metal songs use tabs because they show you the exact frets, string bending, and techniques that make each riff sound brutal. Traditional sheet music can't capture that metal feel!

üìê How to Read Tab - The Basics

The 6 Lines = Your 6 Strings

E|-----| (1st string - thinnest, highest pitch)
B|-----| (2nd string)
G|-----| (3rd string)
D|-----| (4th string)
A|-----| (5th string)
E|-----| (6th string - thickest, lowest pitch)

Numbers = Fret Numbers

The numbers on the lines tell you which fret to press:

E|--0--3--5--|
B|------------|
G|------------|
D|------------|
A|------------|
E|------------|

This means: Play the high E string open (0), then 3rd fret, then 5th fret

üéØ Remember:

  • 0 = Open string (don't press any fret)
  • 1 = 1st fret (closest to the nut)
  • 2 = 2nd fret, and so on...

‚û°Ô∏è Reading Direction - Time Flows Left to Right

Just like reading words, you read tablature from left to right. Each position represents when to play that note.

E|--0--3--5--3--0--|
B|-----------------|
G|-----------------|
D|-----------------|
A|-----------------|
E|-----------------|
Beat 1: 0
Beat 2: 3
Beat 3: 5
Beat 4: 3
Beat 5: 0

üé∏ Playing Multiple Strings

When numbers are stacked vertically, you play them at the same time (like a chord):

E|--0-----------|
B|--0-----------|
G|--0-----------|
D|--2-----------|
A|--2-----------|
E|--0-----------|

This is an Em chord - play all these frets at the same time!

ü§ò Your First Metal Riff

Let's learn a simple power chord progression - the foundation of metal!

E|-----------------|
B|-----------------|
G|-----------------|
D|--3--3--5--5-----|
A|--3--3--5--5-----|
E|--1--1--3--3-----|

This creates the classic metal sound! Press the 6th, 5th, and 4th strings at the same time.

üî• Pro Tip:

Use your index finger to press all three strings at once. This is called a "power chord" and it's the backbone of metal guitar!
